Mastering Word to PDF: Layout Fidelity Tips

Converting a Word document to PDF sounds simple, but layout shifts, missing fonts, and broken images can turn a professional resume into a mess. Here's how to ensure pixel-perfect fidelity.

Use Standard System Fonts

While custom fonts look great, they often aren't embedded correctly. Sticking to classics like Arial, Helvetica, or Times New Roman ensures a clean render on any system.

Leverage High-Fidelity Rendering

PDFMitra uses a "Virtual Width" rendering technique to simulate an A4 page within your browser, capturing every margin and line break exactly as Word intended.

Conclusion

Consistency is key. By using local conversion tools that respect CSS and layout scales, you can rest easy knowing your PDF looks exactly like your Word doc.
